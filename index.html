Occupational TB Surveilance in Low Resource Settings

Who?

  • All health workers
  • Including support staff
  • Clinics, hospitals, any setting caring for patients

What?

  • TB surveilance
  • Symptom screening
  • Testing
  • Treatment
  • Education

When?

  • Baseline
  • Annual

How?

  • Tuberculin Skin Test
  • 10mm = positive in a healthy worker
  • 5mm = positive if immune suppressed

Why?

  • Health Workers Risk is 3-7 times the population risk
  • Treatable
  • Identifying and treating latent TB protects the workforce and patients

What Next?

  • Isolate and treat health workers with active TB
  • Offer treatment to health workers with latent TB
  • Analyze the results and address concersions as failures in prevention

But what about...

BCG? Most positive skin tests in people with past BCG vaccine represent actual TB infection. Without symptoms, this is called “latent TB”.

Community acquired infection? Even in areas with a lot of background TB, health workers are at much higher risk (3-7 times higher).

Medication side effects? The benefits of treating latent TB usually outweigh the risk, but there should be a shared decision making process with the employee.
